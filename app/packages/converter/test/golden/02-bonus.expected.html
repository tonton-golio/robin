Sample Trail Rewards Scheme (Fixture)

Source: sample-meetings/kickoff · followup sample-meetings/followup · prior framing sample-meetings/quarterly-check-in

Invented reward logic for a fictional volunteer program, formalized by Sponsor One on a made-up date and pending sample committee sign-off.

Headline mechanic

  • Split: 60% distance-based · 40% quality-based. Components are independent. (Invented.)
  • Quantum: A made-up points cap → sample maximum ~9,400 points at an invented rate. Scales with the imaginary season length.
  • Deadline: A fictional season-end date is the 100% date for the distance component.
  • Quality bar: ≥80% per section on whatever invented metric fits. Above → reward grows; below → it tapers (gradient, not cliff).

Quality approval — Reviewer

Reviewer One is the invented ultimate quality approver. The sample test: did the section end up safe and clearly marked? Where scores can't be measured cleanly, ≥80% maps to "something comparable" and his made-up judgment drives the call.

Gated section list

Gated — must clear the sample quality bar by season-end:

  • North ridge clearing
  • Creek crossing — excludes bridge rebuild, only decking and signage
  • Switchbacks (Upper + Lower)
  • Drainage (sections/culverts)
  • Blazing (paint / cairn / post)
  • sample-toolshed
  • Made-up signage refresh (also a season priority; scope confirmed in a sample work-day). See scope-sample-signage.
  • Overlook (Reviewer proxy for quality)

Not gating (can lapse without affecting the reward):

  • Kiosk — invented nice-to-have
  • Parking-lot sweep
  • Newsletter write-up
  • Bench refinishing
  • All other sample backlog not above

Operating principle (Sponsor)

"It's allowed to be tough along the way, but not the kind of thing that drops to zero overnight."

The sample reward is designed to be achievable but meaningful — not a make-it-or-break-it cliff.

Open positions

  1. Stretch tier later if the marquee section finishes early. Heard, no commit.
  2. Weather clause — a volunteer asked; sponsor rejected. Volunteer accepts — agreed it would over-complicate.
  3. Crew cascade — sponsor recommends a flat per-shift scheme; the lead hasn't decided.

Status notes

  • Per-section ≥80% metric mapping → folded into the sample charter.
  • Acceleration upside → closed on a made-up date.
  • The lead Lead One has no separate reward line in the sample charter.
