# Beacon — Makerspace Wayfinding

The flagship effort of the makerspace. Beacon tidies up how people *find their way* around the green and the hall — putting up clear signposts, painted markers, path arrows, noticeboards, maps, route labels, posts and tags at each junction — so visitors *follow* the signs instead of asking at the door. The goal: one signposting pass that walks every junction through a quick check and a tidy-up, leaving a clear marker at each one. Target: prove the effort pays off in smoother open days (up to ~40 fewer "where do I go?" questions feeding into [[fete-strategy]]) and have it ready for an autumn open day.

## Open-Day Story

The coordinator's pitch for the open day: a live first-time visitor walked from the gate to the hall, junction by junction, with every marker visible — paired with the practical view: **a few minutes per visitor now (target faster) vs. a steward walking each one over; 40 fewer "where do I go?" questions handled at the gate**. The story shows **per-sign close-up + distance scores on the map**, not a single overall number. See [[briefs/open-day-prep]] and [[footfall-metrics]].

**Phase-two pull.** A committee member is already asking what comes after Beacon — warming on a printed-map handout. Frame: **smoother day AND more visitors**, not just fewer questions.
