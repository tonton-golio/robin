Risk Register — Summer Fete (Makerspace)

Tracks the live risks for the makerspace summer fete on the green. Review weekly at the volunteers' meeting.

Critical Risks

Volunteers ramp too slowly

  • Risk: 2 new volunteers don't get comfortable enough to run 4 stalls by fete day
  • Impact: Miss the planned opening time on the green
  • Likelihood: Medium — a real worry for the coordinator
  • Mitigation:
    • volunteer-onboarding plan: first shift shadowed within a week
    • Walkthroughs from Casey Morgan in the first week
    • Pairing on the first stall setup
  • Owner: Jordan Lee

Wayfinding signs unreadable at a distance

  • Risk: The hand-painted fete signs read fine up close but blur from across the green; visitors miss the stalls.
  • Impact: Can't direct the crowd cleanly on fete day
  • Likelihood: Medium — a layout challenge with no fix yet
  • Mitigation:
    • Parallel workstream — doesn't block stall setup
    • Dedicate focused time from Casey Morgan to the sign layout
    • Consider larger stencilled lettering for the far corners
  • Owner: Jordan Lee

"Big enough" sign size undefined for the long paths

  • Risk: The "readable from across the green" target is clear for a single short path but not for the long looping paths past the pond and the car park — where the right size depends on the view angle. Without a rule, volunteers paint to different mental pictures.
  • Impact: The fete signs end up mismatched in size.
  • Likelihood: High — raised at a meeting; no shared rule exists yet
  • Mitigation:
    • Casey Morgan sets the sizing rule per path before onboarding (letter height by sight distance).
    • Document the chosen rule per path in fete-signage-guide.
  • Owner: Jordan Lee

No regular volunteer check-in (early-warning gap)

  • Status: Cadence agreed at a planning session; both new volunteers started on the agreed date. Downgrade once the rhythm is confirmed running.
  • Risk: A first-time coordinator plus a small crew without a regular check-in means problems surface late.
  • Likelihood: Medium (was High) — structural gap, partly eased by the onboarding plan.
  • Mitigation: Cadence per volunteer-notes-check-ins — 20 min weekly during setup, monthly after.
  • Owner: Jordan Lee

Crew falls through (realized)

  • Status: Roster closed. One person dropped out; another rolled off to a different group. Pairing-to-fill is the operating model now.
  • Risk (active): The coordinator carries more setup load than planned. Two stalls need their work redistributed.
  • Likelihood: Realized. Forward risk = running the fete with a smaller crew.
  • Mitigation:
    • Riley Quinn: active; the gazebo lane is set up.
    • Sam Okafor: starts later; the refreshments lane.
  • Owner: Jordan Lee

High Risks

Single key-holder bottleneck

  • Risk: Pat Nguyen is the only person with keys to the storage shed and the power box
  • Impact: Crew blocked on getting kit out, the gazebo blocked indefinitely
  • Likelihood: Medium — reliable but with a real workload
  • Mitigation:
    • Riley Quinn shadows Pat Nguyen; write down the open-up steps before fete day
  • Owner: Jordan Lee / Riley Quinn

Mixed-stall refunds get complicated

  • Risk: The combined raffle-and-tombola stall needs partial refunds; the cake stall is sold-as-is. One refund rule for both adds complexity.
  • Status: A ruling settled on cash-only, no-refund by design. Risk reduced but not gone.
  • Likelihood: Low (decision made). Residual risk on visitor goodwill.
  • Owner: Jordan Lee / Casey Morgan

Footfall estimate quality

  • Risk: Dana Reyes's footfall guesses are rough notes, not counts. The budget needs a credible visitor number.
  • Impact: The treasurer doesn't trust the figures
  • Likelihood: Medium — a rough sense exists but needs checking
  • Mitigation:
    • Work with Dana Reyes to get last year's gate tallies
    • Run a quick head-count at the spring open day for comparison
  • Owner: Jordan Lee / Dana Reyes

Treasurer-budget uncertainty

  • Risk: The treasurer might hold spend if the year's takings look thin.
  • Impact: The crew could shrink, the gazebo hire constrained
  • Likelihood: Medium — the last review went fine but follow-through is uncertain
  • Mitigation:
    • Frame the gazebo as a one-off purchase so it doesn't hit the running float
    • Have a plan for a smaller fete if needed
  • Owner: Jordan Lee

Power supply stress

  • Risk: The green's single outdoor socket under load — the urn, the PA, and the fairy lights all drawing at once and tripping the breaker
  • Impact: The fete is a small share of the socket's limit, but a trip mid-afternoon hurts.
  • Likelihood: High — happening in setup tests; mitigation underway
  • Mitigation:
    • A staggered switch-on plan reduces the peak draw
    • Riley Quinn moves the urn to the hall's indoor socket
  • Owner: Pat Nguyen / Jordan Lee

Path lighting on the first evening

  • Status: ✅ Resolved — path lighting restored after the timer was reset. green-lighting-fix closed.
  • Residual: A spare lantern set stays boxed by design.
  • Owner: Jordan Lee (closed)

Stall-plan churn friction

  • Risk: The coordinator moves the stall map often, breaking Casey Morgan's setup plan.
  • Impact: Slows setup, creates friction
  • Likelihood: Medium — a recurring pattern
  • Mitigation:
    • Version the stall map as the crew grows
    • Flag layout changes in advance
  • Owner: Jordan Lee / Riley Quinn

Monitoring Risks

Stall rules not aligned with the layout

  • Risk: Many setup complaints trace to unclear stall rules rather than bad signs. If the rules aren't written down, the layout can't meet them.
  • Impact: Setup feels chaotic, the check-ins fill with noise
  • Likelihood: High — flagged at a review
  • Mitigation:
    • Casey Morgan runs a rules-alignment session
  • Owner: Casey Morgan / Jordan Lee

Volunteer pushback

  • Risk: A few long-time stall-holders resist the new layout; some set up where they always have.
  • Likelihood: Medium — a supportive committee, but individual pushback
  • Mitigation: Keep the walk-throughs frequent, celebrate the wins out loud.

Side-event distraction

  • Risk: A tempting craft-fair invitation pulls focus from the fete
  • Likelihood: Low — currently just a passing idea
  • Mitigation: Stay focused on the fete.

Copycat fete risk

  • Risk: The fete is just stalls and goodwill, no special kit. The next village over could copy it.
  • Likelihood: Low — the green's location and regulars form a moat
  • Mitigation: The regulars become the moat.

Budget overrun

  • Risk: A bigger crew plus the gazebo hire exceed the fete float
  • Likelihood: Low — the projection sits comfortably under the plan. See fete-budget.
  • Mitigation: Watch the gazebo-hire cost. See fete-budget

Footfall-count blocker (planning runs blind without it)

  • Risk: Without a real footfall count, every planning step runs blind. The visitor target can't be checked.
  • Impact: The catering order slips and cascades.
  • Likelihood: Medium — an explicit blocker; one volunteer's first job.
  • Mitigation:
    • Riley Quinn's first-week task.
    • If it slips, Casey Morgan pairs to unblock.
  • Owner: Riley Quinn / Jordan Lee (escalation)
