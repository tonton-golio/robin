Open-Day Status Self-Check

Purpose: refresh the handbook on what's actually true for the autumn open day. The handbook holds a snapshot that may now be stale. This brief is a question list the coordinator can walk through so the handbook can update.

How to use: pick 3–5 questions per sitting if walking the whole list is too much. Questions are ordered by how load-bearing they are for the open-day story.

Section 1 — signposting pass / one-walk flow

The handbook believes:

  • A two-stage layout: Check → Tidy-up. A deferred set: Route tags, Hazard notes, Map drafter, Photo boards.
  • A first try on the bench reuses one marker template heavily; the "all fresh" framing was aspirational.
  • Several pieces — a first pass at junctions, a fallback marker, a tidy-up pass, a changes side-list, a single-walk route, a saved map — are all not up yet on the green.
  • A layout-lock target sits before the build slot, owned by a volunteer.

Questions:

  1. Where is the layout lock right now? Is it actually locked, or is "this week" still the target? What's the smallest piece that needs to land before the new volunteers walk in?
  2. Of the missing pieces — which do you intend to land now vs. later vs. defer?
  3. The "maybe just one big map" thought — pursue, park, or noise?
  4. The volunteer's first day — what do they need to walk in useful on day 1? Pre-reading? A live route? A list of layout choices already made?

Section 2 — readability

The handbook believes:

  • Sign scores: Main gate 84.2% / Pond loop 79.5% / Car park 72.1% / Hall door 77.8% close up.
  • The footfall counts are stale; fix-footfall-counts is P0; first task for the volunteer.
  • Path lighting is a bigger open-day worry than sign quality.
  • The wide-map photos have no shared sizing rule.

Questions:

  1. Has the wide-map sizing rule been settled with Casey? If yes, what's the call (letter height by sight distance)? If no, when?
  2. Current path-lighting status — fixed, or still in triage? What's the run-time?
  3. Per-sign commitment — will the open day show a number per sign, or one overall number? Does the committee know which?
  4. Distance-first sizing — still the main lever, or has anything shifted?

Section 3 — open-day walk

The handbook believes:

  • Walk shape: a gate-to-hall route + visible markers, a single map handout, on the open day.
  • The story depends on the finishing touches + route quality, not full photo-board coverage.
  • A few dry runs with the committee late in the window.

Questions:

  1. Walk route selection — your shortlist had a few options. Have you picked, or is it still a longlist? What are you optimizing for?
  2. Before-and-after photo log — is the noticeboard log real, or still planned? Who owns it?
  3. Committee dry-run cadence — one dry run, two, weekly? What does the committee need to see before the live walk?
  4. What story does the committee chair need? Has anything become clearer since the last review?

Section 4 — visitor story

The handbook believes:

  • The footfall guesses were rough notes, not counts.
  • A "fewer questions at the gate" target feeds the open-day narrative.
  • Need real counts + a head-count study to be credible.

Questions:

  1. Footfall follow-up — have the real counts come in? What's the current best estimate per route?
  2. Minutes per visitor vs. a steward per junction — do you have a defensible number yet? If not, what's blocking?
  3. Trial weekends — producing usable evidence, or still rough feedback only?

Section 5 — crew readiness

The handbook believes (post-reshuffle):

  • Two new volunteers onboarding. One = Storage → setup → footfall counts. The other = a refreshments lane.
  • A handy-work volunteer joins later.
  • Roster closed; no refill for the slot that fell through.

Questions:

  1. A day-1 doubt — your reflection flagged a comparison-driven doubt. Resolved, or carried into onboarding?
  2. The handy-work volunteer's lane — what's the first concrete job? Pair, or independent?
  3. First-month checkpoint — what signals would tell you "reopen the sign-up" vs. "stay the course"?
  4. Check-in cadence — set up to run weekly check-ins from day 1?

Section 6 — next season

The handbook believes:

  • A next-season plan to be written during the finishing-touches window.
  • A placeholder list of candidate jobs.
  • An open question: a tempting craft-fair invitation.

Questions:

  1. Have you started sketching next season? What do you already know belongs in it?
  2. The craft-fair invitation — has it moved from passing idea to a real decision?
  3. A post-window job — same shape as planned, or has anything changed it?

Section 7 — committee / personal load

The handbook believes:

  • The treasurer conversation has been parked for weeks.
  • A reflection nudge is due.
  • The coordinator is the layout bottleneck.

Questions:

  1. The treasurer conversation — happened, or still parked? What's the smallest unblocking move?
  2. Layout bottleneck — how are you planning to share the load during onboarding?
  3. What you've been quietly worrying about — anything not surfaced that's load-bearing?

How to capture answers

Either:

  • Voice note: dictate a recording and run the ingest flow — answers land as a note + handbook updates.
  • Conversation: walk this brief in a normal sitting; updates land directly.

Suggested cadence: Section 1–2 first. Section 3–4 next. 5–7 as time allows.

Source: a handbook-state snapshot + verbal updates.
